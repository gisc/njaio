Focus

Make AI technologies and their underlying working concepts and impact relatable to younger students so that they can recognise AI in their daily lives, identify patterns, understand fairness and safety, and engage with simple perception and decision scenarios.

Learning Outcomes

1. Human‑Centred Mindset & Everyday AI

  • Identify where smart things show up (eg speakers, robotics, recommendations).
  • Practise safe choices (ask adults; do not share personal information).

2. Ethics, Safety & Fairness

  • Recognise unsafe scenarios (running to road; oversharing).
  • Choose the safer AI action and explain why (eg care, safety, kindness).

3. AI Techniques & Applications (age‑appropriate)

  • Use patterns (emojis/shapes/sounds) to 'teach' a simulated robot (eg card‑sorting).
  • See that sensors help machines perceive (eg camera, microphone).

4. AI System Design (problem scoping → iteration)

  • Imagine a helper robot; explain what problem it solves and for whom.
  • Give simple feedback: “What could make it kinder/safer?”

Sample Paper

Format (45 minutes)

  • Section A: 20 MCQ questions [60%]
  • Section B: 2 structured questions [40%]
