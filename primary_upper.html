Focus

Allow students to engage AI technologies using simple rules and data activities, evaluate AI outputs, practise managing risks (ethics, bias, privacy), and create simple informed designs and/or prototypes with justification.

Learning Outcomes

1. Human‑Centred Mindset & Everyday AI

  • Differentiate automation vs AI; give real examples (eg vision, speech, translation).
  • Explain “data in → pattern out”; list limits (mistakes, missing context).
  • Evaluate an AI app’s benefits and risks (who benefits? who might be left out?).

2. Ethics, Safety & Fairness

  • Spot bias in AI examples (eg grading by handwriting, image mis‑categorisation).
  • Suggest mitigations (eg collect more diverse data, focus on correct answers).
  • Practise responsible use (eg citing AI help, checking facts, privacy basics).

3. AI Techniques & Applications

  • Distinguish rules vs learning from data.
  • Build a tiny rule‑based classifier (block or logic) and test with counter‑examples.
  • Run a no‑code 'train/test' demo (images or text sentiment) and discuss errors.

4. AI System Design (problem scoping → iteration)

  • Scope a school or societal problem (food waste, lost‑and‑found); list data needed.
  • Sketch architecture (inputs → model/rules → outputs → user).
  • Run iteration: test, notice failure cases, refine rules or data.

Sample Paper

Format (60 minutes)

  • Section A: 30 MCQ questions [60%]
  • Section B: 2 structured questions [40%]
